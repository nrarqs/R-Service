🔧 R-Service: Because German cars are complicated.
Look, I love BMWs, Audis, and Mercs as much as the next guy. But let’s be honest: when a warning light pops up or you hear a weird "clunk" on your E60, it’s usually the start of an expensive headache.

I built R-Service because I wanted a tool that actually understands the "personality" of these cars. It’s not just a code reader—it’s a digital buddy that knows exactly where these engines like to fail.

🚗 What’s the deal with this app?
I’ve spent way too much time on forums and in the garage. This app is basically all that "tribal knowledge" packed into one place.

It knows your engine: It doesn't give you generic advice. If you have an N47, it’ll talk to you about the timing chain. If you’re in a 3.0 TDI, it’ll warn you about those injectors.

Plain English: You don't need to be a certified mechanic. Just type "my car is shaking" or "it sounds like a tractor," and the app tries to figure out what's wrong.

The "Hidden" Stuff: It covers those annoying problems that aren't always on the dashboard—like the E60 sunroof drains that love to drown your trunk electronics.

🛠 How it works (The simple version)
Pick your car: Choose your brand and find your specific engine.

Describe the drama: Type in the symptoms like you're explaining it to me over a beer.

Get the verdict: The app scans its brain for known issues and tells you what to check first.

🎯 Why I made this
Mostly because I wanted to help fellow owners avoid the "parts cannon" (you know, when you keep changing parts hoping one fixes the problem). This is about diagnosing smart, not hard.
